How to Spot Poor Plastering Before It Becomes a Costly Problem

Bad plastering doesn't always announce itself immediately. A substandard finish can look acceptable on day one — only to crack, bubble or crumble months later. Knowing what to look for early can save you thousands.

The most common red flags include hairline cracks running diagonally from window corners (a sign of improper setting compound), a hollow sound when tapped (indicating delamination from the substrate), uneven sheen under raking light (missed sanding between coats), and visible joins through a painted finish (poor taping technique). Any one of these warrants a closer look from a licensed plasterer before you proceed with painting or tiling.

In Melbourne's climate — with significant temperature swings between seasons — poorly bonded plaster is especially vulnerable. If you're purchasing a property or accepting a builder handover, always check walls in natural raking light, ideally early morning when sunlight angles across the surface.

Venetian Plaster vs. Standard Plaster: Which Is Right for Your Melbourne Home?

Both finishes have their place — the right choice depends on your budget, timeline, and the feel you're going for. Here's a plain-English breakdown from our plasterers who've applied both across hundreds of Melbourne homes.

Standard plasterboard (also called drywall or gyprock) is the workhorse of Australian residential construction. It's fast to install, cost-effective, and takes paint beautifully when properly set and sanded. For new builds and renovations where budget is a priority, it's the sensible choice.

Venetian plaster is a hand-applied, multi-coat lime or acrylic-based finish that creates a distinctive marble-like depth and sheen. It costs more — typically 3–5× the price of standard set — but it's genuinely timeless. It also offers better moisture resistance, making it ideal for feature walls in bathrooms and living areas. If you've seen it in a luxury Melbourne home and wondered what it was, that's probably it.

What to Expect When Plastering a New Build in Melbourne

Building a new home in Melbourne's south-east? Here's the full plastering timeline your builder might not spell out — and why the sequence matters for your final finish.

Plastering a new build happens in stages. First comes the frame inspection, then plasterboard is fixed to the studs and ceiling joists. After that, all joins, corners and screw heads are taped and set — typically two to three coats with sanding between each. The whole process on a standard four-bedroom home takes four to seven working days. Rushing any stage — especially drying times — is where problems begin. A good plasterer builds in time for the product to cure properly, particularly in Melbourne's cooler months.

5 Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Plasterer in Melbourne

Not all plasterers are created equal. Before you hand over a deposit, make sure you're asking the right questions — these five will sort the professionals from the cowboys.

  1. 01. Are you licensed? In Victoria, plasterers must hold a domestic building licence for jobs over $10,000. Ask for their licence number and check it on the VBA website.
  2. 02. Do you carry public liability insurance? Any damage to your property during the job should be covered by the tradesperson's own policy, not yours.
  3. 03. Can I see recent local references? A confident tradesperson will have no hesitation putting you in touch with recent Melbourne clients.
  4. 04. Is this a fixed price or hourly rate? Fixed-price quotes protect you from bill shock. Always get it in writing.
  5. 05. Who does the actual work? Some contractors quote and then subcontract the job. Know who will be on-site.
